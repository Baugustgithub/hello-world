Procurement Criteria
Answer a few questions to determine the appropriate procurement method.
Consider the Total Contract Value (TCV) - including initial contract period, optional renewals, and potential total spend.
What makes something "complex or specialized"?
Complex or specialized purchases typically include:
  • Items requiring significant customization or configuration
  • Services requiring specialized expertise, certifications, or unique qualifications
  • Procurements where factors beyond price (quality, implementation approach, experience) are critical
  • Solutions where technical approach or methodology significantly impacts outcomes
Standard items/services are those with clear, widely-accepted specifications that can be accurately compared primarily on price.
Note: This classification affects which procurement method is recommended. Complex/specialized purchases typically use methods that evaluate quality and approach, not just price.
What qualifies as a "single source" procurement?
A true single/sole source situation exists when:
  • Only one vendor can provide the exact item or service needed
  • The item is only available from the original equipment manufacturer (OEM)
  • Required compatibility with existing systems can only be met by one vendor
  • Proprietary technology or unique expertise is only available from one source
Preferences for a specific vendor, prior relationships, or convenience are not valid justifications for single source procurement.
Note: Single source claims require detailed written justification and market research documentation to be approved by Procurement Services.
What qualifies as an emergency?
An emergency is a situation that creates an immediate threat to public health, welfare, safety, or public property, such as:
  • Natural disasters or events causing immediate safety hazards
  • Serious equipment failures affecting critical operations
  • Situations that would result in significant financial loss to the university
  • Circumstances where there is insufficient time to use normal procurement methods
Tight deadlines, poor planning, or administrative convenience do not constitute emergencies.
Note: Emergency procurements must be documented and justified to Procurement Services. They are subject to review and may be rejected if they don't meet emergency criteria.

Procurement Process Overview

Follow these general steps to complete your procurement:

  1. Prepare a detailed specification of your requirements
  2. Obtain necessary approvals and funding confirmation
  3. Contact your procurement team to initiate the process
  4. Develop evaluation criteria if applicable
  5. Work with Procurement Services to finalize the appropriate method
  6. Document the procurement process and decisions
